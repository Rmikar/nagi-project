# Inheritance of Intellectual Property and Assets

Nagi considers how culture can be passed to future generations while protecting both creative work and everyday life.

A creator's name, context, intention regarding alteration, and material security are protected. Opening a work does not mean erasing the voices of creators or communities so that it can be used freely.

## Principles of inheritance

- Respect a person's wishes during life and the agreement of those concerned.
- Make public, private, and time-limited publication available as choices.
- Widen routes for education, research, and preservation.
- Attach provenance and context to derivative use.
- When openness becomes extraction, prioritize suspension and review.

Large assets and cultural resources need not remain closed within private gain alone. They can be inherited as social trusts or commons.

> Inheritance is not fixing the past in place.  
> It is passing a person's or a community's breathing into the future without harming it.
