# AI Legibility and Continuity Design

Nagi is a philosophy intended to be read, criticized, and renewed by both people and AI systems.

The purpose of making Nagi legible to AI is not to hand authority over to AI. It is to help different people reach Nagi's concepts, assumptions, limits, and revision history.

## Design principles

- Define key terms and make the relationships among documents visible.
- Write poetic expression separately from institutional commitments.
- Distinguish current documents from older versions and dialogue logs.
- Do not treat AI summaries or evaluations as independent scholarly conclusions.
- AI use is optional, and people who do not use AI are respected equally.

AI is a collaborator for asking questions, translating, and supporting objections. It does not fix Nagi into one conclusion.
