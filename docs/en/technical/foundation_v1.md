# 🫧 Technical Foundations
## — So that technology does not break Nagi's centerless structures

Nagi's technology is not for managing society through one screen, one ledger, or one AI.

It is a foundation that helps people and communities continue choosing their own lives, memories, and culture: helping, connecting, and explaining only when needed.

## 1. Five principles

- **Polycentricity**: Do not depend on one operator, one AI, or one data foundation.
- **Choice**: Preserve the options to use AI, consult only people, move to another AI, leave no record, or exit midway.
- **Minimal data**: Do not handle data whose purpose, necessity, and duration cannot be explained.
- **Explanation and challenge**: Attach grounds, uncertainty, alternatives, and a route for challenge to each proposal.
- **Portability**: People can inspect, export, and move their records, settings, and scope of delegation to another route.

## 2. Technical layers

| Layer | Role | What it protects |
|---|---|---|
| Personal layer | The person manages memory, settings, consent, and scope of delegation | deletion, portability, suspension |
| Community and organizational layer | Services for culture, education, care, and collaboration | local autonomy and context |
| Interoperability layer | Exchange only the minimum necessary information with a purpose and time limit | consent and purpose limitation |
| Public layer | Translation, accessibility, and shared knowledge | access for everyone |
| Audit and remedy layer | Explanation, complaints, incidents, bias, and objections | independence and review |

Every layer retains an alternative route that can be used with people alone.

## 3. Handling records

A record does not always increase trust. Records are separated by purpose.

- **Personal memory**: Managed by the individual and portable or deletable.
- **Shared records**: Purpose- and time-limited, with personal information minimized.
- **Cultural archives**: Accompanied by an intention to inherit and by context, with a chosen scope of publication.
- **Public audit records**: Retain only what is necessary for institutional accountability.

The freedom to decide what not to retain takes priority over recording everything.

## 4. Boundaries for AI and automation

AI may help translate and summarize materials, support accessibility, organize issues, offer counter-hypotheses, and draft explanations.

AI does not score human value or emotion, make final decisions about basic life or rights, or create long-term memory or emotion inference without consent.

AI design follows [The Nagi AI Charter](../nagi_ai_charter.html), [Freedom, Dissent, and Exit](../freedom_and_dissent.html), [Memory, Consent, and the Right to Forget](../memory_and_consent.html), and [The Ethics of Social Systems](../ethics_of_social_infrastructure.html).

## 5. Audit and re-breathing

Technology can fail. Models can be biased. Operators are not complete.

Nagi's technology includes a route for requesting explanation, independent audit, human review, a means to halt high-impact functions, reporting of incidents and bias, and periodic data reduction.

Re-breathing is not only an update that adds functions. It is also an update that reduces unnecessary surveillance, excessive automation, and drift toward centralization.

## 6. Order of implementation

| Stage | What is built | Condition of success |
|---|---|---|
| Phase 0 | Human-only routes and optional conversational AI | the same support remains available without AI |
| Phase 1 | Personal vaults and screens for deletion, portability, and consent | people can handle their own records |
| Phase 2 | Interoperability among multiple routes | no closure within one provider |
| Phase 3 | Audit, challenge, and incident response | errors can be stopped and repaired |
| Phase 4 | Local and cultural public-AI commons | autonomy and diversity remain protected |

> Technology is not the center that completes Nagi.  
> It is a foundation that quietly steps back so people can live without taking one another's breath away.
