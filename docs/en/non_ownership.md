# Institutionalizing Non-Ownership

Nagi's Non-Ownership does not mean possessing nothing.

What it questions is the point at which land, knowledge, culture, foundational technologies, and similar shared conditions of life become powers through which a small number of people can dominate others.

## What is protected

Private life, homes, memories, everyday belongings, and the personal dimension of creative work are protected around the individual's choice and security.

## What is opened

Educational resources, cultural archives, public infrastructure, natural resources, and foundational technologies are moved away from monopoly and opened to paths of use and inheritance.

## Roles

The roles of use, care, inheritance, derivation, and circulation are distinguished, with responsibilities for explanation and care attached.

A steward is not a ruler. A steward temporarily takes responsibility for care and explanation, and prepares a condition in which something can be passed on.

> Non-Ownership exists to loosen structures in which one person having too much makes it impossible for others to breathe.
