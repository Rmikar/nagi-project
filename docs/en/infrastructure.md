# Social Infrastructure and the Role of AI in Nagi

Social infrastructure is not a conspicuous instrument of domination. It is the quiet support that allows everyone to continue living.

Water, electricity, communication, transport, health care, education, culture, places of refuge, and consultation routes are protected as public foundations. They are not closed around the interests of a few; they require long-term care and accountability.

AI can help with translation, accessibility, information organization, and finding bias.

But connection to AI must not become a condition of public participation. Human-only routes, local support, and offline paths remain. No single AI or provider should control society's information and judgment.

Transparency in infrastructure does not mean exposing private life. It means that operators can explain what they manage, why they decide as they do, and how they receive objections.

The principles through which social infrastructure and AI avoid turning attention, behavior, and relationships into resources—and instead build in explanation, challenge, exit, alternatives, and the ability to stop—are developed in [The Ethics of Social Systems](ethics_of_social_infrastructure.html).
