# Deepening the Principles of Nagi

This chapter carries Non-Ownership, Resonance, and Breathing into real institutions.

Non-Ownership protects security in everyday life while loosening domination through monopoly.

Resonance is not consent or popularity. It is relating while differences remain, and receiving opposition and silence rather than removing them.

Breathing brings rest, recovery, care, culture, and time across generations back into social life.
