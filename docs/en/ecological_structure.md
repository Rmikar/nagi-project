# Nagi Ecological Structure

The environment is not a background for human use.

It is a shared condition in which people, animals, plants, water, soil, climate, and the memories of a place are involved.

Environmental data and AI may help identify change and unequal burdens. They do not replace local observation or the knowledge of people directly concerned.

- Do not push environmental burdens onto unseen regions or future generations.
- Use environmental data to support local autonomy and public explanation.
- Do not turn monitoring technology into excessive control.
- Treat scarce resources in light of necessity and long-term effects.
