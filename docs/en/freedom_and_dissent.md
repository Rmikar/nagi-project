# Freedom, Dissent, and Exit

Nagi values Non-Ownership, Resonance, and Breathing. But the moment resonance is compelled, it is no longer resonance.

This document sets the safe ground that must come before Nagi's institutions, AI, economy, and experiments.

## 1. Basic Breath is unconditional

Access to food, housing, health care, education, communication, and culture must not be tied to evaluation, resonance, contribution, participation rate, AI use, or agreement with a philosophy.

No one should have to disclose their emotions or private life in order to receive support. Declining AI or declining a community must not make basic life more precarious.

## 2. The right to dissonance

Opposition, criticism, silence, disagreement, changing one's mind, and refusing to match the mood of a community are legitimate acts in Nagi.

They must not be treated as immaturity or a lack of harmony, and they must not bring disadvantage.

## 3. Freedom of non-participation and exit

People may leave AI conversations, data collection, ledger records, resonance metrics, breath assemblies, or community governance at any time.

Leaving is neither failure nor betrayal. It must not endanger a person's housing, health care, education, or dignity.

## 4. Freedom not to be observed

There is value in allowing emotions, bodies, conversations, movement, relationships, and creative work to remain unobserved.

Observation is permitted only when it is purpose-limited, minimal, explicitly consented to, revocable, accompanied by alternative routes, time-limited, and externally auditable. Emotion inference and voice or facial analysis must not become permanent merely because they are convenient.

## 5. Freedom to refuse AI

People are free not to use AI, to choose another AI, to consult only people, to choose an offline route, and to access, export, delete, or withhold their records.

People who do not use AI must not be treated as uncooperative or less capable of understanding.

## 6. The right to explanation, challenge, and review

Systems that affect people's opportunities, resources, records, or reputations must provide understandable reasons.

People may know what information was used, offer another interpretation, request independent review, and seek correction, suspension, or remedy when an error or harm has occurred.

## 7. Principles that re-breathing cannot weaken

Nagi continues to change. But freedom, dignity, exit, non-observation, and the protection of dissent must not be reduced in the name of updating.

Updates may only strengthen these protections.

These principles apply not only to AI, but to social systems generally: institutions, platforms, markets, media, organizations, and public systems that shape people's choices and relationships.

> Because there is freedom not to resonate, resonance can be real.
