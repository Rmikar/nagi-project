# The Philosophical Background of Nagi

Nagi does not place the state, the market, corporations, or AI at the center of the world.

The world emerges from layered lives, memory, land, culture, relationships, and nature. Institutions exist to listen to these layers, reduce domination, and preserve room for what can be handed on.

Non-Ownership loosens monopoly. Resonance allows people to relate without erasing difference. Breathing returns recovery and circulation to social life.

Nagi is not a completed answer. It is a compass for resisting the urge to fix the future too quickly.
