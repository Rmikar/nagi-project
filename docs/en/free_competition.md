# Principles of Free Competition Beyond Capitalism

Nagi does not seek to eliminate every form of competition.

What it questions is a structure in which competition takes away security and dignity in daily life, leaving only those who win with meaningful choices.

Competition can cultivate creation, discovery, technology, and local ingenuity. For that very reason, society needs a floor that does not exclude people who lose, and boundaries that limit profit gained by damaging others.

## Competition in Nagi

- Keep Basic Breath outside competition.
- Preserve diverse ways of making culture and technology.
- Do not treat temporary popularity or behavior that is easy for AI to read as the only value.
- Do not expel reuse, care, inheritance, and return to local communities from what matters.
- Protect the freedom to step away from competition.

> Free competition is not the freedom to make someone else unable to breathe.  
> It is the room in which different attempts can grow without breaking life.
