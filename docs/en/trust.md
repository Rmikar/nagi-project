# Trust and Governance

Governance in Nagi is not the monopoly of correctness.

It is the condition in which people can know the reasons for decisions, raise objections, and leave when necessary.

Trust grows when roles, reasons for decisions, interests, moments of review, and routes for remedy are made visible.

- Keep decisions with people closest to the matter whenever they can decide them.
- Do not count silence or opposition as approval.
- Give decisions affecting life or rights an independent avenue for review.
- Put time limits on emergency powers.
- Make it possible for human objections to overturn AI proposals.

Systems that coordinate people—institutions, platforms, markets, media, and AI—must embody these conditions of governance in their design, not merely state them as ideals.
