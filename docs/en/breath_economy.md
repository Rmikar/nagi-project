# Nagi Economic Structure

Nagi's economy values circulation that supports life, culture, and nature rather than competition for ownership.

## Basic Breath

Access to food, housing, health care, education, communication, and culture is not a reward for participation or evaluation. It is protected as the foundation from which people can live with security.

## Three circulations

- **Circulation of things**: Value necessity, repair, reuse, and the continuity of local life.
- **Circulation of knowledge**: Pass learning and technology onward while protecting the context of creators and people directly concerned.
- **Circulation of care and culture**: Place rest, dialogue, creation, and quiet witnessing near the center of social life.

## Records and recognition

A community may record gratitude and circulation. But these records must not become scores that decide a person's worth, livelihood, employment, or access to public services.

## Transition

Try on a small scale while preserving explanation, challenge, exit, and review. Do not retreat from existing rights or social protections.
